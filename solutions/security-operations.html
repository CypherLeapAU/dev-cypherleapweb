Security Operations/ Managed Security Services

We Empower your business with 24/7 Security Operations Service that defends you against evolving threats and helps you respond to incidents. Our security awareness training turns employees into your strongest defences.  Our Security Operations Centre (SOC) is a dedicated team  responsible for monitoring and enhancing your organisation's overall cybersecurity posture. At CypherLeap our skilled information security professionals leverage technology and established processes to detect, analyse, and respond to security incidents, aiming to prevent them before they occur.  We gather data from diverse sources, such as your networks, servers, computers, endpoints, operating systems, applications, and databases. The SOC continuously analyses this data, actively monitoring for any indications of suspicious or malicious activity.

Continuous Monitoring 

Continuous 24/7 network monitoring using SIEM tools enables the SOC to receive instant alerts about emerging threats, providing the best opportunity to prevent or minimise potential impacts.

Incident Response

As soon as a security incident is detected, the SOC takes immediate actions such as isolating or disconnecting affected endpoints, halting malicious processes, removing harmful files, and performing other necessary measures to contain the threat.

Social Engineering 

Social engineering tricks people into revealing sensitive information for cybercriminals to access systems. Attackers impersonate trusted figures to steal data.

Cypherleap's Security Operations team monitors for these attacks and provides training to help teams recognize threats like phishing, spear-phishing, vishing, smishing, and quishing.

  1. Phishing: Deceptive links or requests for sensitive data.
  2. Spear Phishing: Targeted, personalized attacks.
  3. Vishing: Phone-based scams to steal info.
  4. Smishing: Fake texts asking for personal info.
  5. Quishing: Malicious QR codes leading to harmful sites.

Security Awareness Training

Strengthening Cybersecurity Through Effective Security Awareness Programs

Human error continues to be one of the most critical vulnerabilities in cybersecurity. Phishing attacks, social engineering, and other methods that exploit human behaviour present significant risks to organisations. To address these challenges, building a robust security awareness program is essential. Partnering with CypherLeap can amplify these efforts by providing the expertise and resources needed for an effective training program.    

The Importance of Security Awareness 

Human Vulnerabilities in Cybersecurity

Despite advancements in technology and security systems, employees often become unintentional entry points for cyber threats. Studies consistently show that human error accounts for a significant share of data breaches, underscoring the need for comprehensive training that equips employees to identify and mitigate potential risks.

01

The Evolving Cyber Threat Landscape

Attackers are constantly refining their techniques to exploit vulnerabilities. Security awareness programs empower employees to recognise and respond to sophisticated threats, reducing the chances of successful breaches. 

02

Meeting Regulatory Requirements 

Many industries have stringent regulations requiring cybersecurity training for their employees. Implementing a thorough security awareness program not only ensures compliance but also strengthens overall security by promoting best practices.
